Online Parent Orientation

  • 2014-2015
  • ELearning, Assessment

Project Info

As part of my job at Oakton Community College, I designed an online parent orientation, including adapting in person parent orientation, establishing desired parent orientation learning outcomes, assessment methods for outcomes, and benchmarking desired outcomes with local and national standards. As part of this, I determined six key learning outcomes:

  1. Know issues experienced by students in the transition from high school and other previous academic environments.

  2. Identify campus resources and how their services contribute to student success.
  3. Know how students can get involved in co-curricular activities.
  4. Understand the importance of student data privacy.
  5. Feel a sense of community/connectedness to campus.
  6. Understand the importance of personal responsibility as it relates to being a successful college student.

The online modules were designed to aling to these learning outcomes by providing information in discrete chunks of informations which built off of knowledge from prior modules.
