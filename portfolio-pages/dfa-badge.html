Design for America Gamification with Badges

  • 2015
  • ELearning

Project Info

I was part of a team tasked with designing a new motivation system for Design for America studios housed in universities across the country. Our badging system was designed to motivate students in the Design for America studios across the country to complete the design process for design-based projects they are completing. Specifically, our badging system used established principles of motivation, practice and feedback, and mastery learning to improve the outcomes for students both in completion and quality of work. The badging system was designed with multiple levels such that each badge can be earned at a bronze, silver, or gold level. This was done in order to motivate students to want to achieve each level of the badge and thus complete the design process more completely. Every DFA team can complete a bronze level badge quickly and easily, motivating them to complete the task. However, as teams work more on the process for each badge, they can earn a silver or gold level badge, rewarding them for taking more time with each step of the design process. This was also done to incorporate practice and feedback as well as mastery of the design process into the badging system. The requirements for earning each badge have been clearly identified and the process streamlined for studio leads to approve the badge, making the system easy to both reward teams for completion and provide feedback without giving a grade or other form of formal critique.

The full design proposal is included in the live preview link with the high level blueprint for how our badging system would work provided both in the master design document and the image on this page. As part of the process we completed all of these steps:

  • Focused on the problem through expert modeling.
  • Designed and user tested prototypes of badges and badge monitoring systems.
  • Created a comprehensive blueprint for the badging system.
  • Participated in regular feedback and critique sessions to iterate on our design.
  • Formally presented the design ideas and proposals to the client.
